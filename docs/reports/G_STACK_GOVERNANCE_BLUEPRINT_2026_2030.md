# G-Stack Civilizational Assurance Blueprint (2026–2030)

## 0) Document control
- Version: 2.0
- Classification: Regulator-shareable (confidential annexes required for sensitive controls)
- Effective window: July 1, 2026 through December 31, 2030
- Intended adopters: frontier labs, critical-infrastructure operators, prudential supervisors, digital regulators, standards bodies, accredited validators

---

## 1) Executive mandate
This blueprint defines a **regulator-grade governance and assurance stack** for frontier AI, AGI-transition systems, and early ASI-risk precursors operating in a multipolar world. It is structured for direct implementation under:

- ISO/IEC 42001 AI management systems
- NIST AI RMF 1.0
- GDPR
- EU AI Act
- SR 11-7 (model risk management)
- Basel III/IV prudential resilience overlays

The operating objective is **civilizational risk reduction with democratic legitimacy preservation**, not merely organizational compliance.

## 3) Detailed technical architecture blueprint

## 3.1 Core services
- **Policy Compiler**
  - Inputs: legal text, standards controls, regulator directives
  - Outputs: machine-checkable guardrails, test cases, enforcement policies
- **Assurance Graph**
  - Directed lineage of data/model/eval/deployment/incident links
  - Merkle-indexed and signed by accountable role keys
- **Risk Stream Processor**
  - Real-time aggregation of model telemetry, abuse signals, economic indicators, threat intel
- **Tripwire Engine**
  - Detects capability jumps, deception signatures, and anomalous autonomy patterns
- **Override Fabric (GROP runtime)**
  - Multi-party, quorum, and time-lock emergency intervention controls
- **Sovereignty Gateway (NSN connector)**
  - Jurisdiction-aware redaction, transfer restriction, and reporting profile handling
- **Evidence Vault (GEA store)**
  - WORM storage with regulator replay APIs and immutable audit indexing

## 3.2 Trust and security primitives
- Hardware root-of-trust and attested execution for training/inference critical paths
- Split-key governance for model release and capability unlock
- Secret-sharing for emergency disable functions (no single actor control)
- Signed “policy snapshots” for post-incident replay
- Canary environments and deception-resistant adversarial probes

## 3.3 Reference deployment topology
- Tier A: Global standards and coordination hub
- Tier B: Regional NSN coordination exchanges
- Tier C: Entity-level local assurance stacks
- Tier D: Isolated emergency command and forensic enclave

## 5) Layer-by-layer implementation specs

## 5.1 GAIRDS spec
- Board AI Risk Committee charter (quarterly), emergency subcommittee (24/7)
- Named accountable executive per frontier model family
- Disclosure matrix: public, regulator, sovereign confidential, treaty-confidential
- Minimum integrity controls: immutable chain-of-custody for model artifacts

## 5.2 GRI spec
- Dynamic risk taxonomy versioned monthly
- Bayesian risk updates with geopolitical modifiers
- Mandatory trigger classes: cyber uplift, bio design enablement, autonomous replication, strategic deception

## 5.3 CEE spec
- Constitutional policy layers: universal rights, national rights, sector mandates
- Hard constraints (non-overridable), soft constraints (override with supervisory sign-off)
- Runtime constitutional adjudication with signed outcome logs

## 5.4 NSNs spec
- Each node: regulator lead, technical secretariat, independent observer council
- Sovereign policy adapters with interoperability test harness
- Bi-directional incident exchange under pre-negotiated treaty schema

## 5.5 CESE spec
- Continuous eval pipelines:
  - deception detection suite
  - autonomous strategy emergence suite
  - jailbreak and model extraction resilience suite
  - high-impact domain misuse suite (bio/cyber/finance/critical infra)
- Re-certification cadence:
  - standard frontier releases: every 90 days
  - material capability increase: immediate re-certification

## 5.6 GROP spec
- Incident classing:
  - T1: localized impact
  - T2: multi-organization impact
  - T3: national critical infrastructure risk
  - T4: civilizational escalation risk
- Authority model:
  - T1/T2 entity response with NSN notification
  - T3 NSN-commanded containment
  - T4 joint multi-NSN override quorum

## 5.7 GHP spec
- Annual governance red-team and “regulatory capture drills”
- Insider misuse simulations and conflict-of-interest audits
- Controls obsolescence scan with mandatory remediation plan

## 5.8 GSRM spec
- Systemic indicators:
  - model monoculture index
  - compute concentration index
  - market reflexivity amplification factor
  - cross-sector automation dependency ratio
- Macroprudential action bands tied to indicator thresholds

## 5.9 GEA spec
- Unified evidence schema IDs for every control and incident action
- Reproducible regulator replay protocol
- Retention tiers: 7-year baseline, 15-year high-risk artifact class

## 5.10 Meta-Endgame spec
- Capability escrow protocols
- Cross-border compute chokepoint governance compacts
- Transition trigger framework for post-human-level autonomy risk

- Execute first cross-border GROP live exercise
- Launch GSRM supervisory reporting feed

## Phase 3 — Hardening (January 2029 to December 2029)
- Mandatory independent validator attestation for all frontier releases
- Annual adversarial governance stress cycle begins
- Introduce dynamic threshold governance (monthly updates)

## Phase 4 — Perpetual assurance (January 2030 onward)
- 24/7 machine-readable supervisory telemetry
- Joint T4 treaty-level command exercise every six months
- Meta-Endgame readiness score integrated into licensing frameworks

## 17) 2030+ continuity guidance
By 2030, organizations should transition from project-style compliance toward **perpetual assurance institutions** with treaty-linked emergency coordination, continuously updated constitutional control sets, and shared civilizational risk telemetry.

The G-Stack is intentionally extensible beyond 2030 to support AGI/ASI governance shifts while preserving legal rights, democratic accountability, and systemic stability.
