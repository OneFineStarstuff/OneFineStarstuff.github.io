Vector Search Tuning — RED: Critical Path Blocker
Status: 60% complete. Was due: Feb 14. Revised target: Feb 28 (+14 days).
Root Cause: 3 Edge-Case Retrieval Failure Patterns
  • Pattern 1 — Multi-hop cross-document reasoning. Accuracy: 71.2% (target 95%). Example: "What are the conflicting obligations between the 2024 vendor agreement Section 12.3 and the MSA Exhibit B?" Retrieval pulls fragments from both documents but fails to preserve clause-level semantic relationships during chunking.
  • Pattern 2 — Temporal-aware retrieval. Accuracy: 68.4% (target 95%). Example: "What was the board-approved risk appetite statement as of Q3 2025 vs. current?" Vector store treats all chunks as atemporal — no version-aware filtering applied pre-retrieval.
  • Pattern 3 — Negation-sensitive legal queries. Accuracy: 74.8% (target 95%). Example: "Which contracts do NOT contain a force majeure clause?" Dense embeddings poorly represent negation semantics; BM25 sparse retrieval handles negation better but is absent from the current pipeline.
Why This Blocks GA
These 3 patterns are 22% of the Golden Set (110 of 500 queries) and are weighted heavily because they represent query types Legal, Compliance, and Risk users execute daily. Shipping at 91% average with a sub-75% accuracy floor on the most business-critical query class directly contradicts the North Star. Estimated rework cost if shipped: $180K–$240K post-GA. Trust erosion: Legal/Compliance adoption projected to drop from 61% to <30% within 4 weeks.

5Formal Recommendation OPTION B
Recommendation: Option B — Extend Timeline by 2 Weeks
The PMO formally recommends Option B. The rationale is grounded in three factors:
  • 1. North Star alignment. The Board-endorsed directive is unambiguous: quality over schedule. The 95% Golden Set accuracy target is the non-negotiable release gate. Option B is the only option providing high confidence (>90% probability) of meeting this gate. Option A's expected accuracy of 91–93% and 65% probability of missing the gate entirely is incompatible with the directive.
  • 2. Cost discipline. Option B keeps the program within the approved contingency envelope (-$117K total CV vs $120K reserve). Option A blows through contingency by $45K, requiring a formal budget amendment — a governance escalation consuming 1–2 weeks of executive bandwidth.
  • 3. Predictability over optimism. Option B offers a deterministic 2-week slip with bounded cost. Option A offers a probabilistic 0-to-4-week slip with unbounded cost if the accuracy gate is missed. In regulated enterprise programs, predictable outcomes are worth more than optimistic ones.
What we are NOT recommending: No scope reduction. No lowering of the 95% target. No deferral of Legal/Compliance edge cases to post-GA.
ESC Action Required: Approve Option B by Feb 19, 2026 to allow re-baseline and revised GA target (Week 24) communication to stakeholders.
