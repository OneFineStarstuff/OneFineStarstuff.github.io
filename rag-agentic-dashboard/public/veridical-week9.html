5 Visionary Theme — The Knowledge Graph Dividend

From Retrieval to Reasoning: Cross-Document Synthesis Creates Compound Value

Legal multi-hop synthesis marks the transition from document retrieval to document reasoning. Traditional RAG answers “What does this document say about X?” Multi-hop answers “What is the relationship between Document A’s position on X and Document B’s position on Y, and what does that imply for Z?”

General Counsel: “This changes how we approach contract review. The cross-reference capability is genuinely novel.”

Network Effect: Each document added to the knowledge graph increases every existing document’s value by creating new reasoning paths. At 1.31M documents with average degree 4.2, the graph contains ~23M potential 2-hop reasoning paths — a compound knowledge asset that grows super-linearly with corpus size.

Competitive Moat: Replication requires (1) mature knowledge graph with accurate edges, (2) trained GNN, (3) cross-boundary reranker. Estimated 8–12 months and $1.8–$2.4M for a competitor to reach equivalent capability.
