5 — Visionary Theme: Algorithmic Liability

From Accuracy to Accountability: Building Regulatory-Grade AI Outputs

The 92.5% accuracy achievement transforms Project Veridical from a technology implementation into a regulatory asset. As retrieval accuracy crosses the production threshold, the strategic question shifts from "Can the system answer correctly?" to "Can the system prove it answered correctly, and can we defend that proof under regulatory scrutiny?"

This is the domain of algorithmic liability — the legal and regulatory framework governing accountability for AI-generated outputs in regulated industries.

Regulatory Landscape

EU AI Act — Article 52 (Transparency Obligations)
High-risk AI systems must provide machine-readable documentation of reasoning, including data sources, model decisions, and confidence levels. Veridical compliance: Provenance chain v1 + reranker confidence scores provide two-layer reasoning documentation. Full compliance projected Week 9. Enforcement: August 2027.
SEC Proposed Rule 10b-5 (AI-Assisted Financial Analysis)
AI-generated financial analysis must carry audit trails demonstrating sources, reasoning, and limitations. Veridical compliance: Provenance chain + domain-specific confidence thresholds (≥0.80 Legal, ≥0.75 Compliance) provide SEC-grade audit trails. Proposed enforcement: 2027–2028.
