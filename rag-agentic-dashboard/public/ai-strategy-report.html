2.3 Feasibility Assessment vs. State-of-the-Art
Critical Assessment
Overall feasibility: LOW (15-25% probability by 2030). Of the 7 core autonomous capabilities hypothesized for Depths, only 2 (Auto-Scaling, Self-Monitoring) are achievable within 5 years with high confidence. The remaining 5 capabilities face fundamental theoretical barriers, particularly recursive self-improvement and causal reasoning, which require breakthroughs not currently on any credible research trajectory. The system as specified would require ~$2-5B in compute for training alone (10T effective parameters), exceeding current single-model training budgets by 2-5x.

2.5 Risks, Limitations & Safety Analysis
Critical Risks
  • Alignment failure at scale: As demonstrated by P9 and P13, self-correcting systems degrade without external feedback. A Depths-class system operating autonomously faces alignment drift proportional to its autonomy scope.
  • Recursive improvement instability: STaR (P1) and ICLR 2026 workshop (P18) show improvement plateaus in 3-4 iterations. Unbounded recursion remains theoretical. Risk of capability jumps without corresponding alignment guarantees.
  • Compute requirements: Estimated training compute for Depths (~10^28 FLOP) would cost $2-5B at current GPU prices, exceed any single model training run to date, and require 50,000+ H100-equivalent GPUs for 3-6 months.
  • Interpretability gap: Mechanistic interpretability (P16) identifies individual features but cannot fully explain system-level behavior. At 10T parameters, model behavior becomes fundamentally opaque.
Safety Mitigations (Required)
  • Constitutional AI + RLHF: Layer 6 alignment verification via Anthropic-style constitutional constraints and continuous human feedback loops. Not sufficient alone but necessary baseline.[29]
  • Formal verification layer: Verified bounds on action space. Each autonomous action must pass through a formally verified safety checker (TLA+ / Alloy specifications for critical paths).
  • Kill switch architecture: Hardware-level interrupt (not software-bypassable) with <100ms latency. Multi-party authorization for restart. Cryptographic attestation of shutdown execution.
  • Capability evaluation gates: Pre-deployment evaluation against dangerous capability benchmarks (METR, Apollo Research). No deployment without passing safety thresholds at each capability tier.[30]
  • Red-teaming mandate: Continuous adversarial evaluation by independent teams (minimum 3 external orgs). Automated red-teaming via multi-agent adversarial probing.
